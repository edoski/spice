# Thesis alignment and independent red-team

Status: independent decision evidence for [Prototype and choose temporal evaluation
and thesis-evidence semantics](https://github.com/edoski/spice/issues/48). It approves
no `K`, window, metric, tolerance, model, or thesis claim. Production, corpora,
artifacts, Obsidian, and remote state were not changed.

## Verdict

The lean defensible thesis route is a clean fixed-`K` extension, not a numerical
reproduction of the professor paper. Its primary evidence can be an exhaustive census
of every eligible closed-parent origin in predeclared named windows. A small paper
bridge still has value: report one explicitly secondary mean-origin base-fee-savings
view, disclose realized fixed-`K` durations and waits, and state the method differences.
Rebuilding the paper's 12/24/36-second Poisson experiment or a second seconds-bounded
model surface does not earn enough distinct value for this bounded thesis.

The red-team rejects six shortcuts:

1. The paper's 36-second choice cannot select clean-contract `K` or a practical
   tolerance. The paper says preliminary realized savings motivated stopping at 36
   seconds, and it evaluates the deliberately selected 9 November 2025 trace. That is
   outcome-informed evidence, not a pre-outcome design rule.
2. A seconds-bounded comparator cannot be created by dropping fixed-`K` predictions
   whose targets happen to exceed a deadline. That conditions on realized cadence and
   creates unavailable-action/fallback semantics after prediction. A genuine seconds
   contract needs a separately predeclared action set, target, artifact, and scorer.
3. Offline whole-block `gas_used` is not transaction gas. A gas-weighted transaction
   ratio is valid only with a real, predeclared, action-invariant request-gas vector.
   Unit or constant gas reduces to a fee-per-gas ratio of sums and must be named that.
4. The ratio of sums is first an exact finite-window descriptive ratio. Calling it a
   ratio of expectations or long-run spend requires an explicit future process and
   exposure measure. It is never automatically an expected finite-workload ratio.
5. Offline inference failures cannot be dropped before model scoring. Every
   structurally eligible origin must yield one action or invalidate the evaluation.
   Serving availability stays a separate conditional funnel.
6. Old deadline/fallback-rate recommendations are superseded by the approved issue-46
   fixed-`K` contract. Offline structural exclusions and serving availability remain
   valid, but deadline overflow is not a model outcome.

## What the paper does and does not support

The professor paper's temporal experiment has useful thesis anchors:

- compare a selected future fee opportunity with immediate execution and hindsight
  best;
- preserve chronological roles, training-only transforms, and one sealed testing opening;
- report an economic view beside predictive accuracy;
- explain that base fee per gas is a scoped proxy rather than complete transaction
  cost.

It does not supply authority for the clean contract. Its experiment uses random
two-hour trace windows, Poisson requests at `0.05/s`, 50 repeats, separate 12/24/36
second models, an immediate-next-block inclusion assumption, one selected day, and one
fitted model per condition (local `ICDCS_2026.pdf`, pp. 7-10). Its figures do not define
the exact economic reducers or error bars. Its generic `profit` wording omits revenue,
priority fees, gas workload, inclusion, and latency utility. The selected fixed-`K`
forming-block contract deliberately answers another question and must say so.

The smallest honest bridge is therefore:

- one secondary `mean_origin_base_fee_savings_fraction_vs_immediate_k0` on the same
  clean fixed-`K` origins;
- a paper-versus-clean-contract method table;
- per-chain realized `h -> h+K` duration and scheduled broadcast-wait summaries;
- optional earliest-minimum action accuracy only if the predictive-diagnostics ticket
  retains it for a named paper-comparison purpose.

These views aid explanation. They are not paper reproduction and cannot make old and
new scores directly comparable.

## Leakage and selection audit

Predeclare before any clean-contract internal/final outcome is inspected:

- numeric `K` and its action meaning;
- protocol-regime boundaries and raw role cutoffs;
- eligibility and target-horizon purge rules;
- named windows and every reducer/denominator;
- training seeds and the comparison pairing unit;
- practical non-inferiority tolerance and which evidence role applies it;
- the compact metric surface and any paper-facing view.

Repeated search over a finite validation history can overfit the selection procedure,
not only model parameters ([Cawley and Talbot](https://www.jmlr.org/papers/v11/cawley10a.html));
data-snooping corrections exist because selecting rules after comparing outcomes biases
reported evidence ([White](https://doi.org/10.1111/1468-0262.00152)). Already inspected
paper results and old Poisson outputs stay archival/exploratory. The final period can be
sealed against the **new contract's** scores, but it is not a pristine never-seen raw
history; that limitation must be stated.

Issue 47 is still open. It owns the actual feature span and structural eligibility.
Issue 48 may fix evaluation roles and require that every target `h+1...h+K` remain in
its role, but it must keep first-eligible-origin counts symbolic until issue 47 resolves.
Causal past context may cross a role cutoff; future target facts may not.

Use only the plain roles training, validation, and testing. Testing is one sealed,
strictly later, sufficiently broad corpus role. It may contain multiple predeclared,
non-overlapping, regime-contained reporting windows distributed across the later
calendar tail; those windows are views inside one role, not extra dataset roles. Every
eligible origin in the primary predeclared test range is scored exactly once. The old
300/1,200-block windows span minutes on Avalanche and cannot be the sole broad evidence.
Exact cutoffs and any acquisition need remain with coordinated issue-47/issue-48 work
and cannot be selected from model or fee outcomes. Use all eligible origins per chain;
do not force equal counts across chains.

The completed bounded-period audit provides planning evidence, not approval:

- an earlier capacity calculation used a provisional 1.2-million-origin post-Granite
  Avalanche training prefix, 300,000 validation origins, and a 200,000-origin extra
  development segment, leaving roughly 11.735 million later origins spanning about
  18 December through 17 May under provisional history/context/`K` assumptions;
- a provisional 2-million-origin training cap still leaves roughly 10.6 million later
  origins from early January;
- Granite remains the protocol-regime anchor. A training cap is a compute/ML cutoff,
  not a new protocol checkpoint;
- exact endpoints await issue 47's context/history semantics and the approved `K`.

The owner has rejected any extra post-validation dataset role, so the earlier
200,000-origin segment is capacity arithmetic only, not a role proposal. These
candidate counts show that bounded Avalanche training need not consume its broad
post-training evidence tail. They do not
approve a cap, allocation, or window. Ethereum and Polygon cannot be forced to the
Avalanche count without starving their tails or changing calendar exposure, so every
chain must report its own span and count.

Much of the existing tail has already been inspected. It can serve validation evidence,
but it cannot honestly be called a pristine sealed test. A genuinely sealed testing
role likely requires a new contiguous suffix acquired only after issue 47, issue 48,
numeric `K`, and all test views freeze. Polygon especially needs a newer suffix if
“broad” means evidence across months.

The old 06/07 route selected 27 representative non-overlapping windows in each
fee/volatility quartile, producing 216 views per chain and 648 overall. That
outcome-conditioned representative selection cannot define the primary headline.
Exhaustive coverage of the predeclared testing range remains primary. Predeclared
fee/volatility strata and old 300/1,200-block aggregations may survive only as explicitly
secondary descriptive views if the owner finds distinct thesis value.

## Denominator and naming audit

For raw integer target base fees `B_i` (immediate), `R_i` (selected), and `O_i`
(hindsight minimum), a common baseline denominator makes the accounting auditable:

```text
finite savings ratio    = sum(B_i - R_i) / sum(B_i)
finite regret ratio     = sum(R_i - O_i) / sum(B_i)
finite opportunity      = sum(B_i - O_i) / sum(B_i)
savings + regret        = opportunity
```

With declared transaction gas `g_i`, multiply every term by the same `g_i` before
summing. Do not substitute whole-block gas. Preserve numerator, denominator, unit,
origin count, and undefined zero-denominator status. A finite-window ratio
`sum A_i / sum B_i`, a future-process ratio `E[A]/E[B]`, and `E[A/B]` are different
targets ([Gneiting](https://doi.org/10.1198/jasa.2011.r10138)). Ratio inference can be
ill-behaved near a noisy zero denominator ([Fieller](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.2517-6161.1954.tb00159.x));
SPICE should avoid manufacturing bounded symmetric intervals.

Use target-opportunity names, not transaction-cost or profit names. Offline `R_i` is
the historical fee at the intended target, not achieved inclusion. Receipt-backed
serving accounting uses actual inclusion separately. Never condition offline metrics
on a receipt, successful broadcast, or serving availability; complete-case conditioning
can select a different population
([Hernan, Hernandez-Diaz, and Robins](https://doi.org/10.1097/01.ede.0000135174.63482.43)).

## Ties, waiting, and availability

Raw fee equality defines the minimizing action set. A later tied minimum has zero
regret and is fee-optimal, while the approved lexicographic training label still chooses
the earliest minimum. Report:

- tie-aware fee-optimal rate over every eligible origin;
- unique-action accuracy only where the minimizer is unique, with that denominator and
  the tie count visible;
- any earliest-minimum accuracy only as a distinctly named paper/label diagnostic.

Scheduled waiting is fully observed under issue 46: `wait_blocks=k`; for `k>0`, trace
wait seconds run from parent `h` to observation of target parent `h+k`, and for `k=0`
they are zero. It is not receipt latency or target-block displacement. No survival model
is warranted because fixed `K` bounds and observes every scheduled wait. Receipt absence
remains a serving outcome, not a censored offline model wait.

Structural coverage should report candidate origins, eligible origins, and named
exclusion reasons. Serving should report a separate conditional funnel such as
snapshot/request, inference/snapshot, target-parent-opportunity/inference,
broadcast/opportunity, and receipt-observed/broadcast. None changes the offline model
denominator or proves inclusion at the intended target.

## Dependence, pairing, and uncertainty

Adjacent origins share up to `K-1` target rows and fee dependence may last longer.
Timestamp duplicates are distinct block opportunities. The offline key is
`(content_bound_corpus_id, chain_id, block_number)`; live decisions separately
retain `(h, hash(h))`. Do not deduplicate or jitter same-second origins.

For model comparison, use the same eligible origins and compute paired per-origin
differences. Equal numeric training seeds do not make two architectures stochastic
pairs; seed sensitivity and temporal pairing are separate. Diebold-Mariano comparison
allows serially correlated loss differences
([Diebold and Mariano](https://doi.org/10.1080/07350015.1995.10524599)); a process-level
interval would need a declared HAC long-run variance or joint block bootstrap, not an
IID origin standard error. No such inference is required for the lean route.

Recommended labels are exact and modest:

- `finite-window census; no interval` for exhaustive origin scores;
- `training-seed range` for fitted-model sensitivity on fixed data;
- `named-period variation` for the displayed chronological periods.

Do not call Poisson repetitions, overlapping origins, seeds, periods, or chains one
flat independent sample. A practical-equivalence rule on the finite validation census
is deterministic non-inferiority, not a significance test. One-sided
non-inferiority is the relevant direction: a lean model may be materially better, and
that should not disqualify it as two-sided equivalence could.
